🚀 Excited to share my latest open-source project: DotEnvNest! 🚀

As a developer, I’ve always found it frustrating to securely manage and share .env files across different machines or with team members. Sending them over Slack, Discord, or email is a huge security risk, and manually copying them is just tedious.

That's why I built DotEnvNest — a secure, seamless platform and CLI tool to manage, synchronize, and share your environment variables! 🔐

🌟 What is DotEnvNest?
It's a powerful central vault for your environment variables. Whether you are a solo developer switching between laptops, or a team collaborating on a project, DotEnvNest ensures your .env files are always synced and secure.

🔥 Key Features:
- 💻 Powerful CLI: A complete suite of commands to push, pull, view, diff, find, and securely share your .env files straight from your terminal. 
- 🤝 Secure Sharing: Share your .env files with teammates via their email. You can grant 'read' or 'edit' access.
- 🔄 File Variants: Easily manage .env.local, .env.production, etc. The CLI maps and organizes them automatically.
- ⚙️ CI/CD Ready: Integrate it into GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, or Vercel effortlessly using an automation token.

🛡️ How Secure is it?
Security was my #1 priority. Your environment variables are encrypted using industry-standard AES-256-CBC encryption. The decryption requires a personal 6-digit PIN that only you know. Not even the database administrator can read your secrets!
